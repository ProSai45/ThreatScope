❌ What went wrong

▼

The attack succeeded because the victim trusted the email sender without verifying the domain. The urgency of "24-hour expiration" bypassed normal security reasoning, and the professional email formatting created a false sense of legitimacy. The credential harvesting page was a pixel-perfect clone of the real login portal.

⚔ How the attacker succeeded

▼

The attacker used typosquatting (c0mpany vs company), emotional manipulation through urgency, and authority impersonation by posing as the IT department. The attack infrastructure included a cloned login page with SSL certificate and a real-time credential relay to access the victim's account within seconds of submission.

🛡 How to prevent it

▼

Deploy DMARC/DKIM/SPF email authentication. Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all accounts. Train employees to verify URLs through hover inspection. Establish a direct channel to IT for suspicious email reporting. Use hardware security keys for critical system access.
